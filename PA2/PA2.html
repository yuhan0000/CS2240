A Brief history Of Hotwheels

The video above shows a simple yet extensive of Mattel Hotwheels' history that started in 1968.


The first release of Mattel Hotwheels are as shown on the image above, better known as the "Sweet Sixteen". With the distinct look over their rival Matchbox, Hotwheels offered more vibrant colors and realistic and funky car designs which successfully enticed the youth back then.



For the first 10 years of production of the 1:64 scale cars, all its wheels have the distinct "redline" shown above. This was then used as a signifier that the hot wheels you might have right now is an old-first-gen castings. Values have been sckyrocketing since. At the time being, the wheels design and performance was unrivalled, having a wider and smooth base made the cars roll faster on the orange tracks.

After the 10th year of production Mattel has decided to discontinue the "Redline" wheels and create a new, better, faster, sturdier wheel.



The TREASURE HUNTS

"premiums on a basics"

Every year Mattel produces about 519 million diecast cars, to add some spice in the market, they decided to insert approx. 10,000 premium cars inside the basic packaging. Finding a treasure hunt is a rare occurence, and if you do so, consider yourself bery lucky. Treasure Hunts was officially released by Mattel in the year 1995 and has continued to release upto this year, 2022.


Left- Treasure Hunt, Right- Basic Line.

The Main Difference between the TH and Basic is the paint quality and the micro details of the casting, as well as the wheels are now rubber and the whole casting is made out of full metal (Basic Lines are metal but with plastic bottoms and plastic wheels).



The Basic Line

"The Dollar Hobby"

Every year 519 million castings are made by mattell to cater all interested kids in cars and for the collectors of this hobby. Every month there are different castings released and they are all called "cases" Ranging from Case A upto Case Q, each filled with 72 cars per case and might contain one of those Treasure Hunt castings.
